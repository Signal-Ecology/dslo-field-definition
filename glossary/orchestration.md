# Orchestration (DSLO Glossary)

## Definition
Orchestration is the deterministic coordination of agents, processes, and system components under the governance of DSLO’s semantic substrate.

It ensures that all operations preserve meaning and respect invariant boundaries.

## Purpose
- Enforce invariant gates.
- Maintain semantic stability across multi‑agent systems.
- Govern system growth deterministically.
- Prevent drift during autonomous modification.

## Properties
- Substrate‑aligned  
- Invariant‑checked  
- Deterministic  
- Drift‑resistant  
- Context‑bounded  

## Orchestration Functions
- Agent coordination  
- Semantic stability protocols  
- Growth pattern enforcement  
- Merge‑time drift prevention
